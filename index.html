The Monkees Are Here!

The Monkees are an American rock and pop band originally active between 1966 and 1971, with reunion albums and tours in the decades that followed. Since 1967 and the release of Last Train to Clarkesville, The Monkees have been cemented in the landscape of American pop culture. The success of the show led to the actor-musicians becoming one of the most successful bands of the 1960s. The Monkees have sold more than 75 million records worldwide making them one of the biggest selling groups of all time with international hits, including "Last Train to Clarksville", "Pleasant Valley Sunday", "Daydream Believer", and "I'm a Believer". This site honors their over six decade long career by exhibiting the ledgendary quartet's work.

DAVY JONES

Before assuming the role of lead vocalist of the group, Davy was successful in his own right having been the star of west end productions and television, Davy served as front man penning some of the Monkees most iconic hits such as I'm a Believer.

MIKE NESBITT

.Texan born Mike Nesbitt also took a lead role in the construction of the Monkee's musical aesthetic, and engaged in many colloboartions beyond the Monkees writing for Linda Ronstadt among others and engaged in alot of philantropic work.

MICKEY DOLENZ

L.A native Dolenz was born to a show biz family and began his career as a child star in the 50's on the popular TV show circus Boy. Even after the roaring success of the Monkees, Dolenz thrived on stage and screen and continued to make music throughout.

PETER TORK

Hailing from Mansfield, Connecticut, Peter Tork was always a highly gifted musician learning the keyboard, bass, hardsicord and acoustic from a yound age and was always passionate about music. Being a prominent local musician before joining the Monkees, Tork too an active role in the songwriting of the Monkees biggest hits.
